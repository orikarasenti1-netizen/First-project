Why most freelancers undercharge

⏱

They forget non-billable time

Proposals, client calls, admin, invoicing, and professional development all take time you can't charge for. A 40-hour week typically yields 22–26 billable hours.

💸

They skip business costs

Software subscriptions, equipment, health insurance, professional development, and slow months can easily add $5,000–$15,000 per year to your real cost of operations.

📉

They compare to salaries

A $100K employee costs their employer ~$140K in total. As an independent freelancer, your rate needs to cover everything an employer would otherwise pay for.
